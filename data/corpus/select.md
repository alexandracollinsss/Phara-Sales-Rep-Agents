# SELECT Trial — Semaglutide 2.4 mg Cardiovascular Outcomes (Novo Nordisk)

## SELECT: Semaglutide in Overweight/Obesity With CVD (NEJM 2023)

**Citation:** Lincoff AM, Brown-Frandsen K, Colhoun HM, et al. Semaglutide and cardiovascular outcomes in obesity without diabetes. *N Engl J Med*. 2023;389(24):2221-2232. **PMID:** [37866322](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37866322/)

**Design:** Multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; **17,604** adults ≥45 years with BMI ≥27 and established cardiovascular disease but **without diabetes**.

**Intervention:** Semaglutide 2.4 mg once weekly vs placebo; median follow-up **39.8 months**.

**Primary endpoint:** Composite of death from cardiovascular causes, nonfatal myocardial infarction, or nonfatal stroke (MACE-3).

**Key results:**
- MACE occurred in **6.5%** (semaglutide) vs **8.0%** (placebo); **hazard ratio 0.80** (95% CI 0.72–0.90; p<0.001).
- **20% relative risk reduction** in MACE.
- Mean weight change at 104 weeks: −9.39% vs −0.88% (placebo).
- Discontinuation due to GI events more common with semaglutide.

**Clinical takeaway:** First major trial demonstrating CV benefit of an anti-obesity medication in non-diabetic patients with established CVD. Important for formulary discussions when physicians prioritize **Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4 mg)** in ASCVD patients without diabetes.

**Fair balance:** SELECT does not include tirzepatide; **SURPASS-CVOT** will address tirzepatide CV outcomes in T2DM. Cross-trial comparison is indirect only.

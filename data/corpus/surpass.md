# SURPASS Program — Tirzepatide in Type 2 Diabetes (Eli Lilly)

## SURPASS-2: Tirzepatide vs Semaglutide 1 mg (Lancet 2021)

**Citation:** Frías JP, Davies MJ, Brown E, et al. Tirzepatide versus semaglutide once weekly in patients with type 2 diabetes. *Lancet*. 2021;398(10313):583-598. **PMID:** [34469728](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34469728/)

**Design:** Randomized, open-label, phase 3 trial; 1,879 adults with T2DM inadequately controlled on metformin (HbA1c 7.0–10.5%, BMI ≥25).

**Interventions:** Tirzepatide 5, 10, or 15 mg once weekly vs semaglutide 1 mg once weekly (40 weeks).

**Primary endpoint:** Change in HbA1c from baseline at 40 weeks.

**Key results (efficacy):**
- All tirzepatide doses met non-inferiority and superiority vs semaglutide 1 mg for HbA1c reduction.
- Estimated treatment difference in HbA1c vs semaglutide: 5 mg −0.19%, 10 mg −0.22%, 15 mg −0.28% (all p<0.0001).
- Proportion at HbA1c <7%: higher with tirzepatide 10 mg and 15 mg vs semaglutide.
- Mean body-weight change: greater loss with tirzepatide 10 mg (−7.6 kg) and 15 mg (−8.4 kg) vs semaglutide (−5.7 kg).

**Safety:** GI adverse events (nausea, diarrhea, vomiting) most common; similar class effect to GLP-1 RAs; dose-related with tirzepatide.

**Clinical takeaway:** In metformin-treated T2DM, tirzepatide demonstrated superior glycemic control and greater weight reduction vs semaglutide 1 mg at 40 weeks.

## SURPASS-3: Tirzepatide vs Insulin Degludec (Lancet 2021)

**Citation:** Ludvik B, Giorgino F, Jódar E, et al. Once-weekly tirzepatide versus once-daily insulin degludec in patients with type 2 diabetes. *Lancet*. 2021;398(10300):671-684. **PMID:** [34469711](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34469711/)

**Design:** 1,444 adults with T2DM and BMI ≥25 on metformin ± SGLT2i; insulin-naïve; HbA1c 7.0–10.5%.

**Key results:** Tirzepatide 10 mg and 15 mg superior to insulin degludec for HbA1c and body-weight reduction at 52 weeks; greater proportion achieved HbA1c <7% without weight gain.

## SURPASS-4: Tirzepatide vs Insulin Glargine in High CV Risk (Lancet 2021)

**Citation:** Del Prato S, Kahn SE, Pavo I, et al. Tirzepatide versus insulin glargine in type 2 diabetes and increased cardiovascular risk (SURPASS-4). *Lancet*. 2021;398(10313):1811-1824. **PMID:** [34774199](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34774199/)

**Design:** 2,002 patients with T2DM, elevated CV risk, on 1–3 oral agents; treat-to-target insulin glargine.

**Key results:** Tirzepatide 10 mg and 15 mg superior to insulin glargine for HbA1c and weight at 52 weeks; hypoglycemia rates lower with tirzepatide.

**Note:** Dedicated CV outcomes trial for tirzepatide is **SURPASS-CVOT** (ongoing/completion per registry).
